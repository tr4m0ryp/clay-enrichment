## 1. Avelero Company Profile

### Overview

Avelero is a fashion-tech company that provides a digital product passport (DPP)
platform built specifically for fashion and apparel brands. The company enables brands
to launch EU-compliant digital product passports in days rather than months. Founded
by Rafael Mevis (raf@avelero.com), the company is headquartered in Europe and operates
as a SaaS platform.

Website: https://www.avelero.com
App: https://app.avelero.com
Social: LinkedIn (@avelero), X (@avelerodpp)

### Core Product: Digital Product Passport Platform

Avelero's platform creates product-level digital records containing material origins,
environmental impact data, and end-of-life options. Consumers access passports by
scanning QR codes on product labels or packaging.

Key capabilities:

- Passport Designer: Customizable templates with full control over typography, colors,
  layouts, and content. Brands create on-brand experiences rather than generic
  compliance pages. Modular sections allow embedding care guides, repair services,
  resale options, brand storytelling, newsletter signups, product links, and campaign
  banners.

- LCA Engine: ML-powered lifecycle assessment engine trained on validated open data
  using ISO 14040/14044 methodology. Estimates carbon and water impact from minimal
  input (product category plus basic material information). No environmental science
  background required. Uses cradle-to-gate methodology.

- Data Integration: Accepts Excel, PDF, and direct uploads. API connections to Shopify,
  PLM, and ERP systems. AI-powered data gap detection and auto-completion fills in
  missing product data automatically.

- GS1 Digital Link Standard: All passports use the GS1 Digital Link as the default QR
  code standard. QR codes follow the structure https://yourbrand.com/01/GTIN with
  optional serial number extension /21/SERIAL. This avoids vendor lock-in -- brands can
  change where the URL resolves without changing physical QR codes. A single QR code
  serves both consumer passport access and POS scanning. Built on infrastructure used
  by over 2 million companies globally.

### Value Proposition

Avelero positions itself around three pillars:

1. Speed: "Most brands go from raw data to live passports in days, not months." No
   months-long integration projects required.

2. Post-Purchase Engagement: Passports embed care guides, repair services, and resale
   options that keep customers returning after purchase. The passport becomes a revenue
   and retention channel, not just a compliance checkbox.

3. Regulatory Readiness: Built around EU ESPR compliance requirements. The built-in LCA
   engine eliminates the need for separate environmental assessment subscriptions.

## 2. Target Market Analysis

### Ideal Client Profile

Avelero targets fashion, streetwear, and lifestyle brands that:

- Sell physical products (apparel, footwear, accessories) in the EU market
- Have 20 to 500 employees (mid-market, not enterprise giants with custom solutions)
- Care about brand experience and customer engagement post-purchase
- Face upcoming EU DPP compliance requirements
- May lack internal sustainability or compliance teams
- Value speed of implementation over enterprise customization
- Already use Shopify, standard PLM, or ERP systems

### Why These Brands Need DPP

1. EU Regulatory Mandate: The ESPR textile delegated act is expected in late 2026 to
   early 2027, with mandatory DPPs for textile products entering the EU market by
   mid-2028. Brands that sell in Europe have no choice but to comply.

2. Production Lead Times: Fashion has 6-10 month pre-production lead times. Brands must
   generate DPP QR codes by Q3-Q4 2027 to meet label production deadlines. That means
   decisions about DPP providers need to happen in 2026.

3. Anti-Counterfeiting: Digital passports provide product authentication, critical for
   streetwear and premium brands where counterfeiting erodes brand value.

4. Sustainability Pressure: Consumers and retailers increasingly demand transparency.
   Brands without verifiable sustainability claims risk reputational damage and retail
   delistings.

5. Ban on Unsold Goods Destruction: From July 2026, large companies cannot destroy
   unsold textiles. DPPs support resale, repair, and recycling pathways.

## 4. EU DPP Regulation Landscape

### Regulatory Framework

The Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation (ESPR), Regulation EU 2024/1781,
entered into force in July 2024. It establishes the legal foundation for Digital Product
Passports across multiple product categories. The ESPR replaces and expands the previous
Ecodesign Directive (which covered only energy-related products) to include nearly all
physical products placed on the EU market.

### Timeline for Fashion and Textiles

**Already in Effect:**
- July 2024: ESPR entered into force
- April 2025: European Commission adopted the 2025-2030 ESPR Working Plan, designating
  textiles as a priority product category

**2026 Milestones:**
- July 2026: EU centralised DPP registry becomes operational
- July 2026: Ban on destruction of unsold textiles takes effect for large enterprises
- September 2026: Empowering Consumers Directive takes effect, prohibiting unverified
  environmental claims (greenwashing)
- Late 2026: Joint Research Centre expected to complete preparatory studies for the
  textile delegated act

**2027 Milestones:**
- Late 2026 to Q2 2027: Textile-specific delegated act adoption expected, defining
  exact data fields, technical standards, access rights, and mandatory compliance date
- December 2027: Forced Labour Regulation becomes fully applicable

**2028 Milestones:**
- Mid-2028: Mandatory DPPs required for all new textile products entering the EU market
  (approximately 18 months after delegated act adoption)
- This is the effective compliance deadline for fashion brands

**2030 and Beyond:**
- July 2030: Destruction ban extends to medium-sized enterprises
- Early 2030s: Expanded data fields, broader stakeholder access, integration with
  customs, EPR schemes, and recycling infrastructure expected

### Product Scope for Textiles

Included: T-shirts, shirts, sweaters, jackets, trousers, dresses, underwear, socks,
scarves, gloves, and other garment accessories.

Excluded: Smart textiles, personal protective equipment (PPE), medical devices, raw
textile materials.

### What Compliance Looks Like for Fashion Brands

A compliant DPP must include (based on expected requirements):

- Product identification (unique identifier, GTIN)
- Material composition and origin
- Environmental impact data (carbon footprint, water usage)
- Durability and care information
- Repairability and recyclability information
- Manufacturing location and supply chain data
- Compliance certifications
- End-of-life options (repair, resale, recycling pathways)

Data must be accessible via a standardized digital carrier (QR code or NFC) and hosted
by a certified service provider. Some data will be public (consumer-facing), while
other data will be restricted to regulatory authorities.

### Critical Action Window

Fashion brands must begin DPP preparation by mid-2026 at the latest. Given 6-10 month
pre-production lead times for garment labels, QR code generation must happen by Q3-Q4
2027 to meet mid-2028 compliance deadlines. This means vendor selection and onboarding
should occur in 2026 -- which is now.

## 5. Partnership Opportunities

### EU Regulatory and Standards Bodies

**GS1 (Global)**
- Develops and maintains the GS1 Digital Link standard that Avelero already implements
- Over 2 million member companies globally
- GS1 in Europe actively promotes DPP standards
- Partnership value: co-marketing, standard compliance validation, referral network
- GS1 has published a DPP Provisional Standard specifically for this regulation

**CIRPASS-2 Consortium**
- EU-funded project (May 2024 to April 2027) piloting DPPs across textiles,
  electronics, tyres, and construction
- 13 large-scale pilot projects demonstrating functioning DPPs in real-life settings
- Avery Dennison and circular.fashion are members
- Partnership value: pilot participation, standard-setting influence, visibility
  with early adopters

**European Commission Joint Research Centre (JRC)**
- Conducting preparatory studies for the textile delegated act
- Defines the technical requirements that all DPP providers must meet
- Partnership value: early insight into upcoming requirements, compliance validation

### Industry Associations

**EURATEX**
- Voice of the European textile and apparel industry
- Represents nearly 200,000 businesses (mostly SMEs) employing 1.3 million people
- Over 170 billion EUR annual turnover represented
- Supports DPP introduction under ESPR
- Partnership value: access to EURATEX member companies as prospects, industry
  credibility, participation in working groups

**CISUTAC**
- EU initiative to increase textile circularity in Europe
- Focuses on removing bottlenecks to circularity
- Partnership value: circularity credibility, network access

**Federation of European Sporting Goods Industry (FESI)**
- Represents sporting goods manufacturers in Europe
- Relevant for activewear and sportswear brands in Avelero's target market

### Sustainability Certification Organizations

**B Corp Certification**
- Growing number of fashion brands are B Corp certified (Veja, Ecoalf, etc.)
- B Corp brands are natural early adopters for DPP
- Partnership value: co-marketing to B Corp fashion brands

**GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard)**
- Leading standard for organic textiles
- GOTS-certified brands already collect supply chain data needed for DPP
- Partnership value: GOTS brands have data-readiness advantage, lower friction

**OEKO-TEX**
- Textile testing and certification system
- OEKO-TEX certified brands care about transparency
- Partnership value: pre-qualified audience for DPP adoption

**Textile Exchange**
- Global nonprofit driving climate action in the textile industry
- Annual conference brings together sustainability-focused brands
- CIRPASS-2 presented textile DPP work at Textile Exchange Conference
- Partnership value: speaking opportunities, brand exposure

### Technology Partners

**Shopify**
- Avelero already integrates with Shopify
- Shopify app marketplace listing would drive inbound leads
- Many mid-market fashion brands use Shopify as their e-commerce platform
- Partnership value: distribution channel, co-marketing

**PLM Providers (Centric, Lectra, etc.)**
- Fashion-specific PLM systems hold product data that feeds into DPPs
- Integration partnerships reduce onboarding friction for brands
- Partnership value: data pipeline, joint go-to-market

**NFC/RFID Hardware Providers**
- Physical tag providers that complement QR-code-based DPPs
- Relevant for luxury and premium segments wanting NFC tap experiences
- Partnership value: complete physical-digital solution offering
